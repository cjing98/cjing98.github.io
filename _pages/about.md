I am a Ph.D. student in the Child and Adolescent Development Program in the Department of Human Development and Family Science at Virginia Tech.

I earned my bachelor's degree in Psychology from Northeast Normal University and my master's degree in Psychology from Southwest University in China. My previous research focused on creativity, cognition, semantic networks, and cognitive neuroscience.

My current work examines how children use interactive media and how child-AI systems can support children's creativity and active learning. I am particularly interested in the cognitive mechanisms underlying these processes.

My broader research interests include children's creativity, digital media use, semantic network development, storytelling, and AI-supported learning.
